Why Cybersecurity Matters

In today's digital world, cybersecurity is essential for everyone, not just IT professionals. Understanding basic security concepts helps protect your personal information and digital life.

Cybercrime costs billions of dollars every year and affects millions of people. Learning simple security practices can protect you from becoming a victim.

Common Cyber Threats

Malware and Viruses

Malware is malicious software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access to your computer or device.

Types of Malware

  • Viruses: Programs that attach to other files and spread
  • Ransomware: Software that locks your files until you pay money
  • Spyware: Programs that secretly monitor your activities
  • Trojan Horses: Harmful programs disguised as legitimate software

Phishing Attacks

Phishing is when criminals try to trick you into giving them personal information like passwords or credit card numbers.

Common Phishing Methods

  • Fake emails that look like they come from banks or companies
  • Text messages asking you to click suspicious links
  • Phone calls requesting personal information
  • Fake websites that steal your login details

How to Protect Yourself Online

Strong Password Practices

Using strong, unique passwords is one of the most important things you can do to protect yourself online.

Password Best Practices

  • Use at least 12 characters in your passwords
  • Include uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Never use the same password for multiple accounts
  • Consider using a password manager to generate and store passwords

Safe Browsing Habits

Being careful about what you click and download can prevent many security problems.

Internet Safety Tips

  • Only download software from official websites
  • Look for "https://" in website addresses when entering personal information
  • Be suspicious of unexpected email attachments
  • Keep your software and operating system updated
